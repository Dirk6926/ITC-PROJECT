Discover the roots of Kaamulan

John Doe - April 14, 2024

Explore the history, meaning, and traditions that unite Bukidnon’s seven tribes in one grand celebration of identity and heritage...

Kaamulan Festival is more than just a yearly event — it’s the heart and soul of Bukidnon’s identity. The name “Kaamulan” comes from the Binukid word “amul,” meaning “to gather.” True to its meaning, the festival gathers the province’s seven indigenous tribes — the Bukidnon, Higaonon, Talaandig, Manobo, Matigsalug, Tigwahanon, and Umayamnon — to celebrate their shared culture and traditions. This gathering honors ancestral customs, from age-old rituals to colorful attire that reflect each tribe’s rich history. Through Kaamulan, younger generations are reminded of where they come from, and visitors are given a glimpse of a culture that continues to thrive in the highlands of Mindanao.

Feel the rhythm of culture.

John Doe - April 10, 2024

Dive into the colorful world of Kaamulan’s dances, music, and artistic expressions that bring stories of the past to life...

When the beat of the drums fills the air, Kaamulan comes alive through dance, music, and visual art. Each performance tells a story — of love, harvest, peace, and bravery — passed down through generations. Tribal dancers in handwoven garments move in rhythm with native instruments like agung, kulintang, and dabakan, creating a soundscape that’s uniquely Bukidnon. The colorful floats, handcrafted accessories, and artistic costumes showcase the creativity of the people, blending tradition with modern flair. It’s not just entertainment — it’s cultural storytelling through art.
